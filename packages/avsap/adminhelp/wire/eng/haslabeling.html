Wire - Labeling

Correct labeling on a container can offer important clues about what is recorded on the wire. If you are replacing a container that has labeling information, it is important to transfer this information to the new container or label. Original container labels should be regarded with caution, however, as containers are frequently reused or switched by accident. If possible, verify the validity of the container label. Your recording may have a matrix number imprinted on it, which can be used to reference a catalog entry that can verify the content on the recording. As wire recordings are often not commercially produced, however, you may have to go on good faith that the container label properly identifies the intellectual content of the wire—since there probably will not be additional resources to consult.
