Labeling

If your item has any kind of labeling on the container, the item itself or any related material, we highly recommend that you return to the General tab under Assessments Manager, and enter this information in the notes field.

Unidentified tapes have a tendency to get overlooked or shuffled to the back of the shelf in an institution. Basic information should be recorded on a label both on the tape itself and on the tape's container. Information like title, creator, some kind of alphanumeric identifier (whatever your institution uses, be it accession number, catalog number, and so on), and some kind of date information (whatever your institution uses here—the date the tape was received, the date the tape was recorded, et cetera). Bear in mind that describing your materials, both on the containers and in a catalog, help to keep the materials accessible. When you can't find something, you're less likely to devote time, effort, and funds to its preservation.
