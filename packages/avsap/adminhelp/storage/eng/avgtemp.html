Average Temperature

Regulating temperature is very important for lengthening the viable lives of your audiovisual items. According to the Image Permanence Institute, the following are the optimal temperature ranges for some of the audiovisual media types:

  • Low-use motion picture film should be stored frozen if possible. You can keep these items below freezing in a frost-free freezer. It is best to keep the films double-bagged in plastic bags in order to keep condensation from developing on them. This is best for low-use materials because it is necessary to "stage" them or allow them to slowly come up to room temperature before use. Failure to stage these films can lead to breakage as the film will not be as supple as it needs to be for playback until it comes up to temperature. Frozen film can also accumulate condensation if it is not allowed to come up to temperature slowly, usually over a 24 hour period.
  • If you cannot store motion picture film frozen, it is best to store it in as cold an environment as possible. Ideally film should be stored under 36 degrees Fahrenheit. The closest you can get your film collections to these conditions, the longer the films will be viable.
  • Unlike motion picture film, magnetic video and audiotape should not be stored frozen. Freezing magnetic tape can be damaging to it. That said, cold conditions are good for magnetic tape in terms of prolonging its longevity. Keeping your magnetic tape below 52 degrees Fahrenheit can possibly lead to damage to the tape so it is best to keep tapes above 52 degrees and below room temperature.
  • Like magnetic tape, optical media like CDs and DVDs should be kept cold but not frozen. Keeping your optical media below 70 degrees Fahrenheit is best.

Remember that these are optimal storage temperatures and may not reflect what you are able to do in your storage facilities. The closer you can get to these conditoins the better. Keep in mind that variations in temperature are what we are really trying to avoid. Keeping your materials at steady temperatures, even if these aren't perfect, will still help grant them longer life. You should also consider how to arrange your collections. Since different formats have different needs you may consider how you can store them separately so as to properly match an item with an environment. Don't be discouraged! These conditions should be the goal that you are moving toward, even if you can't always achieve them.
