Security Level

Keeping collections secure often means mediating public access to materials. The more control you have over your audiovisual information, the more you can protect them from damage. Circulating collections take the most abuse and are consequently the most potentially at-risk for damage. For our purposes, we will lay out the following levels of access:

  • High security: these are collections that are kept locked away from public access. These materials are rarely, if ever, used and only by trained staff. The public cannot play back these materials on their own. These materials are at very low risk of theft.
  • Moderate security: these are collections to which the public has mediated access. Users can view or listen to these materials in a monitored environment, such as a reading room or designated viewing/listening space. The public can play back these materials with help from trained staff and are monitored while doing so. These materials are at moderate risk for theft.
  • No security: these are collections that are stored in public areas. The public can take these materials home and play them back on their own equipment in unmonitored environments. These materials are at high risk for theft.
