Fire Suppression

Fire suppression systems are critical in a storage area. There are many types of fire suppression systems, but two of the most common are water sprinkler systems and halon systems. Each has its benefits and drawbacks. Water sprinklers are cheaper and more common. Yet repositories are often concerned about water sprinklers because it is assumed that these systems tend to turn all the sprinklers on in the event of a fire, causing more widespread water damage to collections than necessary. Research has proved this to be untrue, however; in fact, sprinkler systems tend to be able to focus their fire suppression very closely by turning on only a sprinkler or two over the fire (Artim, 1993). Water systems are also cheaper to maintain and upgrade. Halon systems, on the other hand, are preferred by many institutions because halon gas can extinguish fires without leaving any damaging residue. They can be very expensive, however, and replenishing the supply of halon gas is costly as well.

Institutions should not fear water systems. When properly maintained, they can be very effective and minimally damaging to collections. Water is also not damaging to the environment, whereas halon gas is environmentally damaging. Water damage to materials is often not the end of the world for your collection—there are many effective techniques for treating water damage and there are several excellent audiovisual disaster recovery specialists who can help, should your sprinklers douse your materials.

Whatever system you choose for your institution, proper maintenance and upgrades are critical to maintain the long-term effectiveness of your fire suppression system. It is also very important that you check with your local building codes to see what you will be allowed to use in your facility. Some states or counties have laws that restrict the kinds of chemicals that can be used in your fire suppression systems.

Having easily accessible hand fire extinguishers throughout your storage facilities is critical when responding to a fire. Extinguishers should be recharged and/or inspected regularly to make sure they are functional.
