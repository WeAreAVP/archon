Open Reel Video - Storage Container

When storing videocassettes and cartridges, it is best to keep them in inert plastic containers (Texas Commission on the Arts, 2007). This helps to keep airborne contaminants as well as pests off of the videotapes. There are several preservation supply companies that design and sell cases for the more ubiquitous cassette formats such as VHS and mini-DV.

When inspecting older 1" and 2" open reel tape containers, be sure to check the inside of the container for debris. Some 2" quad shipping containers have a spongy lining that deteriorates over time into a powdery and then gummy substance that is very difficult to clean. This can cause damage to the surface of the tape. If you encounter this situation, place the tape (after a thorough inspection) into a clean, debris-free container. Two inch quad tape containers are easily identifiable; they are 15" X 15" X 4" square containers and can weigh 20-30 lbs (Texas Commission on the Arts, 2007).
