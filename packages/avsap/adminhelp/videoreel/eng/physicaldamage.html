Physical Damage

If you load a videocasette with crumpled up or otherwise damaged videotape into a deck, you're asking for trouble because you're significantly increasing the odds of the tape being eaten and/or further damaged by the playback machine. (Sadly, there are no guarantees that even a pristine tape won't be eaten by a machine—this is why we must preserve playback equipment alongside our media.) Open reel tapes are generally easier to inspect than cartridge based videotapes. Look for crumpled tape or popped strands in the tape pack. Tape damage is not limited to the edges of the tape, so look out for wrinkles or damage along the width of the tape.
