Labeling

If your item has any kind of labeling on the container, the item itself or any related material, we highly recommend that you return to the General tab under Assessments Manager and enter this information in the notes field.

Unidentified reels have a tendency to get overlooked or shuffled to the back of the shelf. Basic information should be recorded on a label both on the reel itself and on the reel's container. Information like title, creator, alphanumeric identifier (whatever your institution uses—accession number, catalog number, and so on), and date information (whatever your institution uses—the date the tape was received, the date the tape was recorded, etc.). Bear in mind that describing your materials, both on the containers and in a catalog, helps to keep the materials accessible. When you cannot find something, you are less likely to devote time, effort, and funds to its preservation. If you are discarding an old container which holds labeling information, transfer the information to the new container.
