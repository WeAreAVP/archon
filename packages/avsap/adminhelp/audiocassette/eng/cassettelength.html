Tape Length

Tape length At-A-Glance:

  • Valuable tapes recorded on longer cassette are a higher preservation risk.
  • Commercially produced pre-recorded audiocassettes are generally 30 minutes in length.
  • Blank, recordable tapes tend to vary more—lengths are generally indicated somewhere on the cassette shell.

Cassettes of longer length have thinner tape, which makes them more susceptible to stretching, snapping or other damage during playback. Cassettes that are 90 minutes or longer are also more subject to print-through, in which an echo (either pre- or post-) may be heard when the tape is played.
