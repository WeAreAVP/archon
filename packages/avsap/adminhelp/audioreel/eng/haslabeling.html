Labeling

If your item has any kind of labeling on the container, the item itself or any related material, we highly recommend that you return to the General tab under Assessments Manager and enter this information in the notes field.

Unidentified tapes have a tendency to get overlooked or shuffled to the back of the shelf. Basic information should be recorded on a label both on the tape itself and on the tape's container. Information like title, creator, alphanumeric identifier (whatever your institution uses—accession number, catalog number, and so on) and date information (whatever your institution uses—the date the tape was received, the date the tape was recorded, etc.). Bear in mind that describing your materials, both on the containers and in a catalog, help to keep the materials accessible. When you cannot find something, you are less likely to devote time, effort and funds to its preservation. If you are discarding an old container that holds labeling information, transfer that information to the new container.

Additionally, if you are considering re-housing your tape, you should transfer or retain the labeling and notes (if extant) of the original container. These notes may be helpful to a future researcher.
