Open Reel Audio Tape - Physical Damage

If you load a crumpled or otherwise damaged reel onto a deck, you are asking for trouble. Continued play of an already damaged tape significantly increases the odds of the tape being eaten and/or further damaged by the playback machine. (Sadly, there are no guarantees that even a pristine tape will not be eaten by a machine—this is why we must preserve playback equipment alongside our media.)

If your tape is in the fully rewound or fast-forwarded position—which is recommended—you may see tape leader when you look along the tape path. Leader is generally made of uncoated polyester tape, and is used to protect the tape. As tape is played back, it is most likely to be damaged at the beginning and end of the film. If there are any issues with the playback equipment, the leader would ideally take the brunt of the damage. Damaged leader is easier to replace than unique sections of tape.
