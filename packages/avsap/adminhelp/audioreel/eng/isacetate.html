Open Reel Audio Tape - Acetate Deterioration

These ratings have been adapted from the AvSAP ratings for motion picture film suffering from vinegar syndrome. When film is suffering from vinegar syndrome, we recommend that you freeze the film to slow or halt the decay. Freezing is not recommended for magnetic tape. Freezing tape can cause damage to the information layer of the tape—this is why AvSAP recommends storing acetate-based audiotape in cool, not frozen conditions.

No Deterioration. This tape is suffering from no acetate decay.

Deterioration Starting. Acetate decay is starting. This tape should be monitored and kept in carefully monitored conditions. The tape is flexible, with little (less than 1%) to no apparent shrinkage. There may be a very faint vinegar smell.

Actively Deteriorating. This tape is actively deteriorating. It should be moved to carefully monitored conditions if possible and duplicated. The tape will have a stronger vinegar odor and may exhibit shrinkage (between .8 to 2%). The tape may be able to be accessed and played back in-house by an experienced technician using maintained and functioning equipment. The tape will also exhibit some waviness along the edges; it may curl slightly and resist lying flat (NFPF, 2004).
