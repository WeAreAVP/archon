Open Reel Audio - Appropriate Container

Containers protect the tape from dirt, dust, mold and vermin. These contaminants can damage the surface of the magnetic tape, which can affect its sound quality and even render it unplayable. Ideally, these materials should be housed in archival-quality inert plastic containers to avoid damage from non-archival quality boxes. An audiotape's container should be clean and protect the tape's recording surface. Moldy, damaged, and dirty containers should be replaced.

If your tape is housed in an acid-free paper or plastic container and you're unsure if it is inert or is acceptable for preservation purposes, check the condition of the container. If it is protecting the tape from dust, pests and other contaminants, not shedding or introducing any contaminants through its own degradation and is clean and free of mold or excessive dirt, the container is acceptable for use. Rehousing to a more expensive inert plastic container is not vital in this case. If the tape is housed in anything other than a hard plastic container, e.g. in a cardboard slipcover, or is not sufficiently protected, it should be rehoused in an inert plastic container. Additionally, any reel with a slotted hub should be replaced.
