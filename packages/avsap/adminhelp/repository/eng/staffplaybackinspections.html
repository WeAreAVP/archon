Playback Inspections

Audiovisual materials pose a dilemma to preservation professionals: in order to know for sure the condition of many kinds of AV materials, we must play them. However, playback poses risks to materials that we cannot always anticipate. Even a videotape in pristine condition can be damaged by a playback machine. To further complicate the issue, it is often difficult to predict with visual inspection alone—particularly with magnetic video and audio tapes—which items will have trouble in playback. When using AvSAP, remember that playback assessments for formats like magnetic audio and video tape, which cannot be reliably visually assessed, are more diagnostic but are also more potentially perilous. For this reason, we do not recommend playback assessments unless you are reasonably sure of the physical integrity of your AV items and the proper functioning and cleanliness of your playback devices.
