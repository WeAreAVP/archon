Collections Catalog

If you do not know what is held in your collection, the items may be especially vulnerable to theft, neglect, deterioration, improper storage and deaccessioning. Most institutions have some sort of inventory control or finding aid for items. These may be created for internal inventory control purposes when an item is accessioned into the collection. However, these accession records may not be authoritative, descriptive, or publicly searchable.

Machine-readable media such as videotape, audiotape, and digital files are especially vulnerable if no descriptive or cataloging data are available. These items cannot be visually browsed or skimmed like books, and rely on machines for information retrieval.

In order to make these analog or digital items searchable, it is important to collect information about the object. Information such as a unique identifier, title, creator, date, format, collection size, access restrictions, and use restrictions (based on intellectual property and copyright issues) are key elements for access. At the very minimum, you should consider documenting this information.
