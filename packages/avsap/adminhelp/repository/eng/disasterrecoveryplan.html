Disaster Recovery Plan

A disaster recovery plan establishes how an institution will respond to both large and small scale disasters. This plan delineates, among other things, how you will treat your materials if they are exposed to flooding, smoke, fire, storms, and other disasters. Having a disaster plan in place before a disaster strikes can make your response much more effective and can potentially keep a bad situation from becoming much worse. dPlan is an excellent online tool that can guide you through the process of generating a disaster recovery plan: http://www.dplan.org/. A good disaster plan can also help you to avert disasters by helping you make informed decisions about where to store materials, how to prepare for a fire, and so on. A disaster plan should specify what the various staff members' responsibilities are in the event of a disaster as well as what materials have priority in terms of evacuation and recovery (NFPF, 2004, p.67). Using AvSAP to assess your materials can help you to generate a prioritized list of what materials will need attention in the event of a disaster.
