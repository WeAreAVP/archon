Collection Policy

A collection development policy or mission statement can help an institution determine the kinds of materials it collects, both in terms of content and object type. It can help prevent the institution from collecting materials that would be better suited elsewhere. For example, if you work for the Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library and have a 16mm film about the canyons of southern Utah, you would most likely find that the film does not serve your institution's mission. Additionally, finding a more appropriate home for the film would mean one less audiovisual object for you to store and preserve, which would free up funds and space. Adhering to your mission statement can also help researchers—after all, how many are likely to come to the Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library looking for a film about Utah's canyons?
