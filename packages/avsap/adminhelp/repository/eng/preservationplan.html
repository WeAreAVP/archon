Preservation Plan

A preservation plan is a document that specifies what an institution's preservation needs are and what steps the institution needs to take to address those needs with the resources it has. To be effective, this plan should deal with all of the institution's collections. It should also be a living document—growing over time and as collections are added to the institution. AvSAP can help your preservation plan be more effective by giving you a deeper understanding of what audiovisual collections are most at risk and, therefore, which collections require the most pressing preservation attention.

The Northeast Document Conservation Center's (NEDCC) preservation planning leaflets are an excellent resource for understanding the fundamentals of preservation planning and can be found at http://www.nedcc.org/resources/leaflets.list.php.
