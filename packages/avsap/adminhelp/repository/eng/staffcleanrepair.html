Cleaning Media

Audiovisual preservation requires a great deal of specialized knowledge. Knowing how to clean and repair your media, to the extent that it can be done in-house, is an excellent way to maximize your preservation budget. Some formats, like grooved disc audio media and motion picture film, are very amenable to in-house cleaning. Other formats, like most magnetic video and audio media, are not as easily repaired in-house. Getting this specialized knowledge has traditionally been difficult, but in recent years the internet has become an excellent resource. One example is the "Video Aids to Film Preservation" website, which contains a great deal of images, text, and video showing the user how to use film preservation equipment, do basic repairs, and clean motion picture film. The website can be found at: http://www.folkstreams.net/vafp/. Another excellent resource is the Texas Commission on the Arts' "Videotape Identification and Assessment Guide" at http://www.arts.state.tx.us/video/. This website, which provided a great deal of images to AvSAP, is dedicated to identifying and assessing the variety of videotape formats that are available. The National Film Preservation Foundation has published another good motion picture film preservation resource, the "Film Preservation Guide," which is available as a free download: http://www.filmpreservation.org.

However, nothing can compare with the experience you can gain from taking a hands-on audiovisual preservation workshop. Many are available throughout the year through local and national professional organizations like the Association of Moving Image Archivists (AMIA) http://www.amianet.org/, the Association of Recorded Sound Collections (ARSC) http://www.arsc-audio.org/, the Society of American Archivists (SAA) http://www.archivists.org/, the American Library Association (ALA) http://www.ala.org/, and many more. Consulting the websites and joining the email listservs for these organizations are excellent ways to stay up-to-date on the audiovisual workshops and lectures in your area.

It is also very important to make sure you have appropriate cleaning and repair equipment for your AV media, if you want to do this in-house. As you use AvSAP to assess your materials, we will give you guidance with regard to doing hands-on cleaning and repair work.
