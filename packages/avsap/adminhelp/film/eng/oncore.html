Cores

Ideally films should be stored on cores (not on reels—see below for a discussion of the differences between reels and cores). Films stored on reels or stored not on a reel but without cores are considered higher preservation priorities.

A core is distict from a reel. A reel is a "metal or plastic hub with extended sides between which the film is wound for projection" (NFPF, p. 106). Reels—especially metal, dented or otherwise damaged reels—are NOT appropriate for long-term storage.


Film on a reel compared to film on a three inch, inert plastic core

These cores should be made of an inert plastic material. For 16mm and 35mm it is recommended by the National Film Preservation Foundation (NFPF) that the core is at least three inches wide. A larger core diameter lessens the stress on the film as the film does not have to wind as tightly around a larger core as it would with a small core. Smaller gauge film may be wound on smaller (less than 3") cores.
