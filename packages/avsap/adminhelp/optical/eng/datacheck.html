Optical Media - Data Checks

Data on a CD or DVD, especially a non-replicated recordable/re-writable CD or DVD, is subject to loss over time. All optical media should be regularly checked for data degradation. As "there is no (measurable) deterioration by replay with optical disks" (Schuller, 2008), error checking and testing is a "mandatory part of storing data on a recordable disc" (Bradley, 2006, p. 11).

There are several classes of optical media testers. The most accurate are production-quality, standalone testers which can start at approximately $30,000. The next are mid-range production quality testers, with a starting price of approximately $3,000 (in 2006). These systems require regular calibration and maintenance for consistent accuracy. All mid priced systems should be regularly calibrated against a known standard. Currently, a major manufacturer of such mid range testers is Clover Systems (http://www.cloversystems.com/) (Bradley, 2006). The least reliable are downloadable software program testers. These programs "use a computer's inbuilt CD/DVD drive to measure error in written CD and DVDs" (Bradley, 2006). However, due to several limitations with the software and hardware, they are not recommended for archival purposes.

To reiterate, optical media, especially recordable media, is volatile and subject to sudden failure, so testing is a critical component. In a report published by UNESCO, Bradley (2006) states: "Test your newly recorded discs. If a CD test program is beyond budget, choose a different storage medium" (p. 12). This shows how important it is to test your optical media.
